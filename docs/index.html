Weekly Brief – 2025‑12‑01

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • CodeRED emergency alert system attacked: A ransomware attack, claimed by INC Ransom, targeted the CodeRED alert platform used by municipalities across the U.S., triggering widespread service outages. Some user data (emails, clear‑text passwords) may have been compromised, prompting affected cities to advise password resets. Source: https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/news/2025/11/millions-at-risk-after-nationwide-codered-alert-system-outage-and-data-breach
  • SitusAMC breach endangers major US banks’ data: The consulting/outsourcing firm that serves large financial institutions was breached, potentially exposing corporate documents and client data tied to firms like JPMorgan Chase, Morgan Stanley, and Citigroup. Investigation and law‑enforcement involvement are ongoing. Source: https://www.techradar.com/pro/security/situsamc-hack-may-have-exposed-data-at-major-financial-heavyweights Source: https://www.itpro.com/security/cyber-attacks/wall-street-giants-warned-of-data-exposure-following-supply-chain-attack
  • Canon confirmed among alleged victims of Cl0p Oracle EBS campaign: Canon’s subsidiary was added to the growing list of more than 100 organizations named on the Cl0p leak site following exploitation of an Oracle E‑Business Suite vulnerability. Source: https://www.securityweek.com/canon-says-subsidiary-impacted-by-oracle-ebs-hack
  • Fake job scams distribute “Flexible Ferret” stealer to macOS users: Attackers are leveraging bogus job offers on professional networks combined with fake video updates to trick macOS users into installing a multi‑stage stealer malware, granting long-term access and data theft capabilities. Source: https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/news/2025/12/a-week-in-security-november-24-november-30

Source: https://cyble.com/blog/cyble-weekly-vulnerability-report-november-2025/

Source: https://apnews.com/article/4b1c167746f2c0e7d263be94f0aa94b0

Analyst Comments / Defender Impact Summary

Geopolitical shifts are reshaping the cyber‑threat landscape more rapidly this week. The public acknowledgment by NATO that hybrid and cyber warfare might require proactively defensive or offensive strategies signals a potential escalation in cyber engagements between states. The attack on a U.S. firm for tangential Ukraine‑related ties illustrates that adversaries are widening their target scope beyond direct government or military actors. Organizations should treat indirect associations (e.g. vendor relationships, sister‑city ties, geopolitical alignment) as potential risk vectors. For defenders, maintaining visibility on global tensions and aligning risk assessments accordingly is now as critical as patching known vulnerabilities.
