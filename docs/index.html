Weekly Brief – November 24, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • A large-scale campaign targeting the Gainsight CS integration in the Salesforce CRM ecosystem has been detected, possibly affecting 200+ customer instances. The attacker(s) abused OAuth tokens of the Gainsight-published app to gain unauthorized access to downstream Salesforce customer data. Source: https://www.cybersecuritydive.com/news/salesforce-investigating-customer-connected-Gainsight/806093/
  • A brute-force and login-scanning surge has been observed targeting the GlobalProtect VPN portals of Palo Alto Networks (PAN-OS) systems, with over 2.3 million malicious sessions logged since November 14. This raises concerns about remote access infrastructure exposure. Source: https://cybersecuritynews.com/palo-alto-vpn-under-attack/
  • A campaign by Dropping Elephant (also tracked as Patchwork/APT-C-09) has targeted Pakistan’s defence sector with spear-phishing delivering a Python-based backdoor via MSBuild dropper, using living-off-the-land binaries. Source: https://gbhackers.com/elephant-group/
  • A supply-chain scenario where attackers abused OAuth tokens in SaaS applications (rather than exploiting a vulnerability in the core platform) was confirmed by Google Threat Intelligence Group as a growing trend. Source: https://www.csoonline.com/article/4094506/oauth-token-compromise-hits-salesforce-ecosystem-again-gainsight-impacted.html

Vendor & Security Product Updates

Source: https://www.securityweek.com/cisco-fortinet-palo-alto-networks-devices-targeted-in-coordinated-campaign/

Analyst Comments / Defender Impact Summary

Organizations must regard OAuth and other non-human identity tokens as critical attack surfaces. The Gainsight/Salesforce incident underscores that adversaries are shifting to “trusted app” exploits rather than classic perimeter breaches. VPN and firewall login portals remain high-value targets - the brute-force surge against GlobalProtect/EPS endpoints shows attackers probing for exposed access points. Defenders should prioritise token inventory, integration scope reduction, MFA enforcement, login anomaly detection, and applying the latest patches on network access infrastructure.

Summary

Immediate threats:

  • OAuth token abuse in trusted third-party integrations (e.g., Gainsight/Salesforce) enabling broad downstream access.
  • Massive brute-force login campaigns targeting VPN/remote-access portals (GlobalProtect) exposing credentials and session hijacking risk.

Actionable steps:

  • Audit all third-party SaaS integrations, revoke unused or suspicious OAuth tokens and service-accounts.
  • Enforce MFA and multi-factor controls on remote access portals; reduce external exposure of VPN/portal login endpoints.
  • Monitor login patterns for brute-force attempts and establish anomaly detection on authentication logs.
  • Apply patches and vendor guidance for high-risk appliances (VPNs, firewalls, WAFs) immediately.

Longer-term needs:

  • Adopt continuous monitoring of non-human identities (service accounts, OAuth tokens) and incorporate token lifecycle/permissions into threat hunting.
  • Build operational readiness for AI-driven threat campaigns and integrate detection for automated reconnaissance/exploitation chains.
  • Reassess SaaS supply chain risk - establish granular least-privilege scopes for integrations, enforce vendor/partner resilience controls.
