Weekly Brief – October 6, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • **Asahi Group cyberattack (Japan)**: A ransomware or disruptive attack disrupted ordering and delivery systems across multiple plants, halting beer production and threatening supply shortages. The company is still assessing full recovery. Source: Reuters
  • **Salesforce data theft claims by “Scattered LAPSUS$ Hunters”**: The group claims nearly one billion Salesforce-related records were stolen by targeting downstream client organizations rather than Salesforce itself. Source: Reuters
  • **Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) extended production halt**: After an earlier cyberattack, production remains offline while investigations continue. Source: Cybersecurity Dive
  • **European airport disruption via Collins Aerospace vMUSE**: A cyberattack on Collins Aerospace’s vMUSE system disrupted check-in and boarding infrastructure at multiple major European airports. Source: Wikipedia

Vendor & Security Product Updates

  • Oracle issued an emergency patch for a critical zero‑day in Oracle E‑Business Suite (CVE‑2025‑61882), which had been actively exploited in data theft and extortion campaigns. Source: Oracle
  • Spike in scanning activity targeting Palo Alto Networks login portals: threat intelligence observers reported a ~500% jump in scan attempts on October 3. Source: The Hacker News

Geopolitical & Political Developments

  • In the U.K., a suspect in his 40s was arrested in southern England for a cyberattack that disrupted operations at several European airports, including Heathrow. Source: ABC News
  • Surveys reveal ~30% of business leaders report increased cyberattacks within their supply chains over the past six months, highlighting the rising threat of supply‑chain compromise. Source: The Guardian

Notable Vulnerabilities & Patches

  • CVE‑2025‑61882 (Oracle E‑Business Suite, RCE): A critical, unauthenticated remote code execution flaw, actively exploited prior to patch. Oracle recommends immediate patching. Source: Oracle
  • CVE‑2025‑32463 (sudo chroot privilege escalation): Attackers are actively exploiting this flaw to escalate unprivileged accounts to root. Source: SOCRadar / CISA advisory
  • CISA added several vulnerabilities to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog, including flaws in Linux, Android, Cisco IOS/IOS XE, and GoAnywhere MFT. Source: US CERT / CISA listing

Threat Actors

  • **Cl0p**: Leveraged the Oracle EBS zero‑day (CVE‑2025‑61882) in recent data theft campaigns. Source: SOCRadar
  • **Scattered LAPSUS$ Hunters / The Com**: Claim affiliation with the Salesforce-related theft campaign. Source: Reuters
  • **UNC6040 & UNC6395**: FBI‑named groups targeting Salesforce instances for data exfiltration and extortion. Source: Cybersecurity Dive
  • **FiveHands**: CISA flagged a new ransomware variant “FiveHands” associated with use of SombRAT and publicly available exploitation tools. Source: CISA

Analyst Comments / Defender Impact Summary

Attackers continue to combine zero‑day exploitation with social engineering and supply chain manipulations. The rapid patch release from Oracle underscores how quickly threat actors are weaponizing freshly discovered vulnerabilities. Defenders should assume that any major ERP, identity, or infrastructure component may be under active reconnaissance or exploitation.
The Asahi disruption demonstrates that cyberattacks are increasingly targeting operational/logistics systems—not just data stores—creating real-world supply chain risk. The collated threat actor activity around Salesforce, Oracle, and privileges (sudo) suggests a multi‑vector approach: initial access via phishing or misconfiguration, lateral movement, privilege escalation, and exfiltration.

U.S.-Focused Threat Intelligence Snapshot

  • Organizations using Oracle EBS in U.S. sectors (finance, manufacturing, public) face direct risk from CVE‑2025‑61882 exploitation.
  • U.S. entities leveraging Salesforce must review accounts for signs of compromise tied to UNC6040 / UNC6395, as warned by the FBI. Source: Cybersecurity Dive
  • CISA’s KEV updates raise urgency for patching known exploited CVEs across Linux, Android, Cisco, and GoAnywhere MFT assets. Source: US CERT / CISA
  • Recent supply chain attacks and targeting of procurement infrastructures emphasize the need for visibility into third‑party integrations and APIs used by U.S. firms.

Security Community Trends (GitHub & Twitter/X)

  • The Hacker News reports on a Chinese-linked cybercrime group (UAT‑8099) using compromised IIS servers for SEO fraud as a credential exfiltration vector. Source: The Hacker News
  • GitHub and security blogs are increasingly publishing PoCs and protections around “CometJacking,” a prompt‑injection style attack targeting agentic AI browsers like Perplexity’s Comet. Source: The Hacker News

Emerging Technical Intelligence (Moderate Confidence)

  • Some analysts are observing early signals that hacktivist groups are shifting from DDoS and leaks toward operational technology (OT) targeting in critical infrastructure. Source: arXiv preprint “From Protest to Power Plant”
  • The Beijing Institute of Electronics Technology & Application (BIETA) and its commercial subsidiary CIII are assessed to have ties to China’s intelligence (MSS), including research into steganography and covert communication tools. Source: The Hacker News

Summary

  • Immediate threats: Active exploitation of Oracle EBS zero‑day (Cl0p), sudo privilege escalation (CVE‑2025‑32463), Salesforce compromise by UNC6040/6395.
  • Actionable steps: Urgent patching of Oracle EBS, sudo updates, review Salesforce logs and alerts, inventory supply chain integrations, monitor scan and reconnaissance activity.
