Weekly Brief – September 22, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • Collins Aerospace (unit of RTX) suffered a **ransomware attack** that disrupted airport check-in and baggage-drop services across Europe via its Muse software. Thousands of passengers affected. Source: https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/airport-chaos-highlights-rise-in-high-profile-ransomware-attacks-cyber-experts-say-2025-09-22/
  • Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) had a major hack that halted production for nearly three weeks, causing severe supply chain disruptions. The attack is having large financial impact and widespread supplier risk. Source: https://www.wired.com/story/jlr-jaguar-land-rover-cyberattack-supply-chain-disaster & Source: https://www.theguardian.com/business/2025/sep/20/jaguar-land-rover-hack-factories-cybersecurity-jlr
  • Stellantis detected a breach via a **third-party provider** affecting its North American customer operations. Exposure was of basic contact info; financial or highly sensitive data reportedly not compromised. Source: https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/stellantis-detects-breach-third-party-provider-north-american-customers-2025-09-21/
  • Vietnam’s National Credit Information Center (CIC) is under investigation for a cyberattack likely involving unauthorized access to personal and credit history data. ShinyHunters suspected. Source: https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/vietnam-investigates-cyberattack-creditors-data-2025-09-12/
  • A nearly 200,000‑person data breach at New York Blood Center exposed names, Social Security numbers, driver’s license numbers, bank account details (if used), and medical data. Source: https://www.tomsguide.com/computing/online-security/nearly-200-000-people-hit-in-new-york-blood-center-data-breach-names-ids-ssns-and-more-exposed
  • Iran‑nexus group UNC1549 deployed MINIBIKE malware in a campaign targeting 34 devices across 11 European telecom companies via LinkedIn‑job‑lures. Source: https://thehackernews.com/2025/09/unc1549-hacks-34-devices-in-11-telecom.html
  • CISA published a Malware Analysis Report for “Malicious Listener” malware targeting Ivanti Endpoint Manager Mobile (EPMM), exploiting CVE‑2025‑4427 and CVE‑2025‑4428. This includes advice about detecting, mitigating, and patching. Source: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/analysis-reports/ar25-261a

Vendor & Security Product Updates

  • Ivanti released patches / security advisory fixes for EPMM to address CVE‑2025‑4427 (authentication bypass) and CVE‑2025‑4428 (remote code execution) vulnerabilities. Organizations using these versions are urged to upgrade. Source: https://forums.ivanti.com/s/article/Security-Advisory-Ivanti-Endpoint-Manager-Mobile-EPMM?language=en_US & Source: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/analysis-reports/ar25-261a
  • Security advisories and detection signature releases (YARA, SIGMA) from CISA associated with Ivanti EPMM exploits; detection content distributed. Source: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/analysis-reports/ar25-261a

Notable Vulnerabilities & Patches

  • CVE‑2025‑4427: Authentication bypass in Ivanti Endpoint Manager Mobile API component, allows access to protected resources without credentials. Source: https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/cve-2025-4427
  • CVE‑2025‑4428: Code injection / remote code execution vulnerability in Ivanti EPMM, when chained with CVE‑2025‑4427; allows arbitrary code execution. Source: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/analysis-reports/ar25‑261a
  • CVE‑2025‑10585: A type confusion vulnerability in Google’s V8 engine, exploited in the wild. (Chrome especially) Source: https://securityweek.com/iranian-hackers-target-aviation-and-defense-sectors-in-middle-east/ & original), report mentions that Chrome 140 update patches this zero‑day. Source: https://securityweek.com/iranian-hackers-target-aviation-and-defense-sectors-in-middle-east/

Threat Actors

  • UNC1549 (Iran‑nexus): Active campaign using MINIBIKE malware via LinkedIn job lures targeting European telecom companies. Also uses MINIBUS backdoor in other contexts. Source: https://thehackernews.com/2025/09/unc1549-hacks-34-devices-in-11-telecom.html
  • China‑nexus espionage actor(s) exploiting Ivanti EPMM vulnerabilities to deploy malware / backdoors, including “Malicious Listener” loader/listener malware. Source: https://blog.eclecticiq.com/china-nexus-threat-actor-actively-exploiting-ivanti-endpoint-manager-mobile-cve-2025-4428-vulnerability & Source: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/analysis-reports/ar25‑261a

Analyst Comments / Defender Impact Summary

  • The Ivanti EPMM vulnerabilities (CVE‑2025‑4427 & CVE‑2025‑4428) are especially high risk because they allow **pre-authentication** remote code execution when chained. Organizations with exposed Ivanti EPMM (especially on‑premises) are at serious risk if patches are not applied.
  • UNC1549’s use of social engineering via job lures and leveraging SaaS or cloud‑infrastructure to hide operations makes detection harder; defenders must focus on phishing hygiene, endpoint visibility, and network telemetry to catch early signs.
  • High profile ransomware attacks like the Collins Aerospace incident show disruptive impact beyond financial loss; critical infrastructure (transportation, supply chains) is increasingly targeted, amplifying systemic risk.

U.S.-Focused Threat Intelligence Snapshot

  • CISA’s Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog now includes CVE‑2025‑4427 & CVE‑2025‑4428 (Ivanti EPMM). U.S. entities with exposure are strongly advised to treat these as high priorities. Source: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/analysis-reports/ar25‑261a
  • No public attribution yet in U.S. incidents of breaches directly tied to UNC1549 since the telecom campaign was mostly European‑telecom firms; but related techniques and tradecraft are relevant for U.S. telecom, cloud, and critical infrastructure sectors.
  • Several U.S. healthcare entities continue to be major breach targets; New York Blood Center breach highlights the continuing sensitivity of medical / health‑related data and its potential for downstream misuse (identity theft, fraud).

Security Community Trends (GitHub & Twitter/X)

  • Researchers have published or flagged new detection signatures (YARA, SIGMA) for Ivanti EPMM exploitation; community is rapidly disseminating tools / rules to detect the “Malicious Listener” malware. Source: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/analysis-reports/ar25‑261a
  • In the UNC1549 telecom campaign reports, researchers are calling out the use of Azure‑hosted C2 infrastructure and “job offer / recruiting” themed lures again; these social engineering vectors reappear often and trending. Source: https://thehackernews.com/2025/09/unc1549-hacks-34-devices in 11‑telecom.html

Emerging Technical Intelligence (Moderate Confidence)

  • Some forensic detail from Ivanti EPMM post‑exploit activity shows attackers dropping loader files (e.g. web‑install.jar, ReflectUtil.class, SecurityHandlerWanListener.class) into server /tmp directory. These are used to create listeners/backdoors that accept HTTP requests with embedded payload (Base64 & AES encrypted) to execute arbitrary code. Source: https://www.picussecurity.com/resource/blog/malicious-listeners-ivanti-epmm-cve‑2025‑4427‑cve‑2025‑4428‑backdoors & Source: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/analysis-reports/ar25‑261a

Summary

  • Immediate threats: Unpatched Ivanti EPMM systems remain highly vulnerable to unauthenticated RCE; UNC1549’s recent activity in European telecom sector using MINIBIKE is ongoing and has impact on credential/location exposure.
  • Actionable steps: Patch Ivanti EPMM to latest versions; use IOCs above to search logs; apply detection rules (SIGMA / YARA) from CISA; increase phishing vigilance; monitor cloud/SaaS‑infrastructure for suspicious job/recruitment themed lures.
  • Longer‑term needs: Strengthen supply chain risk monitoring; improve visibility and telemetry across OT/Edge systems; integrate threat actor profile tracking (e.g., UNC1549, China‑nexus) into threat hunting practice; invest in proactive detection of zero‑day exploitation behavior.
