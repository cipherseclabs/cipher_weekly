Weekly Brief – September 17, 2025

Cybersecurity Events & Incidents

  • Emergence of a new ransomware variant named EXTEN that encrypts files by appending “.EXTEN” and provides a ransom note titled “readme.txt”. Victims are instructed not to shut down or reboot devices. Affected OS: Windows. Source: https://www.cyfirma.com/news/weekly-intelligence-report-12-september-2025/
  • Android bulletin: Two Android vulnerabilities—CVE‑2025‑38352 (kernel race condition) and CVE‑2025‑48543 (privilege escalation in Android Runtime)—are confirmed under exploitation in the wild. Source: https://socradar.io/september-2025-android-security-bulletin-cve-2025‑38352‑cve-2025‑48543/

Vendor & Security Product Updates

  • Microsoft’s September 2025 Patch Tuesday: patches for ~80 CVEs; eight rated critical. Notable vulnerabilities include CVE‑2025‑55234 and CVE‑2024‑21907, both zero‑days. Source: https://www.tenable.com/blog/microsofts-september-2025-patch-tuesday-addresses-80-cves-cve-2025‑55234
  • Critical vulnerabilities patched include: CVE‑2025‑54910 (RCE in Microsoft Office), CVE‑2025‑54918 (Elevation of Privilege in Windows NTLM), and other critical RCE or privilege escalation bugs in Windows Graphics / Hyper‑V / Graphics Kernel etc. Source: https://www.crowdstrike.com/en-us/blog/patch-tuesday-analysis-september-2025/

Geopolitical & Political Developments

  • No major new geopolitical cyber‑events in the last 7 days beyond standard state‑actor surveillance and espionage patterns. (Insufficient new public data in this interval.)

Notable Vulnerabilities & Patches

  • CVE‑2025‑38352 (kernel race condition in Android) – under active exploitation. Source: https://socradar.io/september-2025-android-security-bulletin-cve‑2025‑38352‑cve‑2025‑48543/
  • CVE‑2025‑48543 (privilege escalation in Android Runtime) – also under active exploitation. Source: https://socradar.io/september-2025-android-security-bulletin-cve‑2025‑38352‑cve‑2025‑48543/
  • CVE‑2025‑54910 – Critical RCE in Microsoft Office (heap overflow, Preview Pane vector). Source: https://www.crowdstrike.com/en-us/blog/patch-tuesday-analysis‑september-2025/
  • CVE‑2025‑54918 – Elevation of Privilege in Windows NTLM. Source: https://www.crowdstrike.com/en-us/blog/patch-tuesday‑analysis‑september-2025/
  • CVE‑2024‑21907 – a zero‑day patched by Microsoft in September 2025. Source: https://socradar.io/september-2025-patch-tuesday-zero-days-cve‑2025‑55234/
  • CVE‑2025‑55234 – another zero‑day addressed in the recent Microsoft updates. Source: https://socradar.io/september-2025-patch-tuesday‑zero‑days‑cve‑2025‑55234/

Threat Actors

  • Qilin / Agenda – Active Ransomware‑as‑a‑Service operation, using double extortion; written in Golang and Rust; targets include Windows—but also VMware ESXi in some Linux variants. Entry vectors: phishing, exposed interfaces, VPN compromise. Source: https://www.checkpoint.com/cyber-hub/threat-prevention/ransomware/qilin-ransomware/
  • Android exploit activity affecting core OS and runtime via Android‑focused vulnerability exploiters using CVE‑2025‑38352 and CVE‑2025‑48543. Source: https://socradar.io/september‑2025‑android‑security‑bulletin‑cve‑2025‑38352‑cve‑2025‑48543/

Analyst Comments / Defender Impact Summary

  • The rise of EXTEN ransomware adds another tool in the double extortion ransomware landscape. Windows‑based enterprises should ensure backups and system snapshots are protected, and not rely solely on shadow copies (which attackers may delete).
  • The Android vulnerabilities under active exploitation pose a serious risk for devices in enterprise mobility and bring‑your‑own‑device (BYOD) environments. Patch quickly, especially for devices that handle sensitive data.
  • Because several Microsoft zero‑days have just been patched, there is likely a window of opportunity where unpatched systems are highly vulnerable. Ensure patch deployment and potential compensating controls.

U.S.‑Focused Threat Intelligence Snapshot

  • Microsoft vulnerabilities (Office, NTLM, Windows Graphics, etc.) are relevant to a large number of U.S. organizations across public and private sectors. U.S. entities should monitor CISA advisories and ensure alignment with KEV catalog entries.
  • Qilin's RaaS targeting and tools indicate risk to U.S. healthcare & critical infrastructure sectors, especially given the pattern of affiliates choosing high‑value victims.
  • Android devices in U.S. corporate environments are at risk; ensure mobile device management (MDM) systems push patches for the exploited CVEs immediately.

Security Community Trends (GitHub & Twitter/X)

  • Growing discussion around “zero‑day + exploit chain” scenarios (e.g., Android kernel & runtime CVEs) among malware researchers.
  • Increased sharing of IOCs in public GitHub repos like ESET’s and others, especially related to Qilin/Agenda operations. (Hashes, malicious domain lists, etc.)

Emerging Technical Intelligence (Moderate Confidence)

  • Some reports (e.g. Red Piranha) claim use of BYOVD (Bring Your Own Vulnerable Driver) techniques in recent Qilin campaigns to disable or bypass EDR/AV tools. Source: https://redpiranha.net/news/qilin‑ransomware‑all‑you‑need‑know
  • There is moderate confidence that threat actors are increasingly combining Android local privilege issues with kernel race conditions to move laterally on compromised devices. Source: https://socradar.io/september‑2025‑android‑security‑bulletin‑cve‑2025‑38352‑cve‑2025‑48543/

Summary

  • Immediate threats: Devices or systems unpatched for recent Microsoft and Android zero‑day vulnerabilities; organizations vulnerable to Qilin‑type ransomware infiltrations via VPN, phishing or exposed services; Windows systems susceptible to new EXTEN ransomware.
  • Actionable steps: Prioritize patching for CVE‑2025‑38352, CVE‑2025‑48543, CVE‑2025‑54910, CVE‑2025‑54918, CVE‑2024‑21907, CVE‑2025‑55234. Harden remote access (VPN, RDP), monitor for known IPs/hashes. Ensure backups are isolated and immutable where possible.
  • Longer‑term needs: Improve threat hunt capacity; deploy or improve EDR/XDR with detection for file extension renaming, shadow copy deletion; better mobile device security posture; consider BYOVD and vulnerable driver risk in security architecture; increase sharing of IOCs with industry peers.
